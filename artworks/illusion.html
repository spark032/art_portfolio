Illusion

Broken plates, Mirror, Styrofoam board, 2021
H 35" x W 24" x D 20"

Through this sculpture, I explored the similarity I found between superstition and illusion: the delusive nature that makes people strongly believe in something, which is not true or doesn't exist actually. On the black triangular base, I placed the shards of broken plates, which symbolize aegttaem, a superstitious thought. I then put the base in a triangular prism and arranged mirrors inside so that they reflect the patterns of the shards and create repetitive, expansive patterns. These illusive patterns that make the viewer dizzy and confused about what they are really looking at represent superstition— illusive, distorted, and irrational thoughts and ideas that sometimes fill up one’s mind. The mirrors function to expand the illusive patterns beyond the triangular base, symbolizing a gullible, vulnerable human mind that reproduces and deepens illusive and irrational thoughts.
