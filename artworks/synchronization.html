Synchronization

Ceramic, Beads, Spangles, Wire, 2022
H 13" x W 9" x D 7"

This series of two ceramic sculptures represents my traumatic experience of being secretly photographed by men during artistic swimming performances, exploring the distorted male gaze that objectifies athletes behind the sport's beauty. "Sync I" features figures jumping with spangles from my old swimsuit, while "Sync II" shows underwater supporters painted in murky blue, together depicting the hard work required for harmonious performance. The broken mirror pieces along the water's surface symbolize men's distorted gazes that view artistic swimming as visual satisfaction rather than athletic achievement.
