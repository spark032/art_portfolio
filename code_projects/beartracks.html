BearTracks

A Course Planning Tool for Brown University Computer Science Students

About This Project

BearTracks is a full-stack web application that helps Brown University students concentrating in either the A.B. or Sc.B. for Computer Science plan and track their courses across semesters, check course prerequisites, and visualize their concentration requirements. This project was created by Noah Lim, Noreen Chen, Stephanie Lin, and Sue An Park.

Problem & Motivation

The Computer Science department at Brown recently underwent significant changes with new concentration requirements and irregular course offerings. Students struggle to organize and schedule classes across various information sources (Advising Sidekick, Courses @ Brown, CS Advising Ed), creating a confusing and time-consuming planning process. Our solution consolidates all up-to-date information in one interactive platform.

Key Features

  • Drag-and-Drop Interface: Add, move, or remove courses across semesters intuitively
  • Smart Prerequisite Validation: Automatic checking with warnings for invalid course sequences
  • Persistent Course Plans: User authentication with saved progress via Firebase
  • Concentration Requirements Sidebar: Real-time tracking of A.B. and Sc.B. degree progress
  • Course Search & Integration: Direct integration with CAB data for accurate course information
  • Flexible Semester Views: Toggle between 2, 4, and 8 semester layouts
  • Manual Course Entry: Add non-CSCI courses to complete your academic plan
  • Semester Management: Add/remove semester blocks with dropdown selection

Project Impact

Developed by a team of 4 Brown CS students, BearTracks addresses a real need affecting the entire Computer Science department. The tool replaces time-intensive spreadsheet planning and fragmented information gathering with a comprehensive, visual, and interactive platform that makes academic planning more efficient and enjoyable.
